How Spinal Decompression Works

Your spine contains 23 intervertebral discs that act as shock absorbers between your vertebrae. Throughout the day, gravity compresses these discs, causing you to lose up to 2cm of height by evening. This is why you're actually taller in the morning than at night.

Key Finding: Studies show that consistent spinal decompression therapy can increase disc height by 1-3mm per session, translating to measurable height gains over time.

Most Effective Decompression Methods

  • Passive Bar Hanging: Hang from a pull-up bar for 30-60 seconds, 3-5 sets daily. The simplest and most effective method.
  • Inversion Table: 5-10 minutes at 60° angle, twice daily. Studies show measurable spinal elongation.
  • Cobra Stretch: Lie face down, push up while keeping hips on the ground. Hold 30 seconds, repeat 5 times.
  • Cat-Cow Stretch: On hands and knees, alternate arching and rounding the spine. 20 reps daily.

The Daily Decompression Routine

  1. Morning: 5 minutes of cobra and cat-cow stretches while discs are fully hydrated
  2. Midday: 3 sets of 45-second passive bar hangs
  3. Evening: 10 minutes of yoga (downward dog, child's pose)
  4. Night: Sleep on your back on a firm mattress
